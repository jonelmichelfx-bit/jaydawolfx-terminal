LESSON 01 · FOREX BASICS
What Is Forex?
The biggest financial market in the world — and how ordinary people trade it

Forex stands for Foreign Exchange. It is simply the act of buying one country's currency and selling another country's currency at the same time.

Think about it this way — if you traveled from the US to Europe, you would exchange your US Dollars for Euros at the airport. That is forex. The only difference is that traders do this electronically, in seconds, trying to profit from the change in value between currencies.

🌍 The Size of the Forex Market: Over $7.5 TRILLION is traded every single day in forex. That is bigger than the stock market, bigger than crypto, bigger than any other market on earth. It runs 24 hours a day, 5 days a week.
// Why Do Currency Values Change?

Currency values move up and down based on how strong or weak a country's economy is. Here are the main reasons currencies move:

1
Interest Rates: When a country's central bank (like the US Federal Reserve) raises interest rates, their currency usually gets stronger. Why? Because investors from around the world move their money there to earn more interest. More demand for that currency = higher price.
2
Economic Data: When a country's economy is doing well — low unemployment, strong growth, good earnings — their currency tends to rise. When the economy is struggling, the currency falls.
3
World Events & Conflict: War, political instability, natural disasters — these cause traders to sell currencies of affected countries and buy "safe haven" currencies like the Japanese Yen (JPY), Swiss Franc (CHF), or US Dollar (USD).
4
Supply and Demand: Like anything — when more people want a currency, its price goes up. When more people want to sell it, its price goes down.
📖 REAL WORLD EXAMPLE

In 2022, the US Federal Reserve raised interest rates aggressively to fight inflation. As a result, the US Dollar became the strongest it had been in 20 years. The Euro dropped to below $1.00 for the first time ever. Traders who understood this made huge profits by selling EUR/USD (betting the Euro would fall against the Dollar).

✅ Key Takeaway: Forex is simply trading one currency against another. Values move based on interest rates, economic health, and world events. Your job as a trader is to predict which direction a currency is heading before it gets there.
Quick Check: What is the MAIN reason the US Dollar got stronger in 2022?

LESSON 02 · FOREX BASICS
Currency Pairs — The Building Block of Forex
Why currencies always trade in pairs, and how to read them

In forex, you never buy just one currency. You always buy one and sell another at the same time. This is called a currency pair.

Every currency pair looks like this: EUR/USD = 1.0850. This means 1 Euro costs 1.0850 US Dollars right now.

// How To Read a Currency Pair
📖 BREAKING DOWN EUR/USD

EUR = The Base Currency (the one you are buying or selling)

USD = The Quote Currency (the one you are pricing it in)

1.0850 = It costs $1.0850 to buy 1 Euro

If EUR/USD goes UP from 1.0850 to 1.0950, the Euro got stronger (you profit if you BOUGHT)

If EUR/USD goes DOWN from 1.0850 to 1.0750, the Euro got weaker (you profit if you SOLD)

// The Major Pairs — Most Traded in the World
EUR/USD
Euro vs US Dollar — the most traded pair. Moves a lot during US and European sessions. Affected by Fed and ECB decisions.
GBP/USD
British Pound vs Dollar. Called "Cable." Very volatile — moves fast. Affected by UK economic data and Bank of England decisions.
USD/JPY
Dollar vs Japanese Yen. Yen is a safe haven — goes UP when the world is scared. Affected by Bank of Japan policy.
USD/CHF
Dollar vs Swiss Franc. CHF is another safe haven. Switzerland stays neutral in wars so money flows there during crises.
AUD/USD
Australian Dollar vs USD. Called "Aussie." Moves with commodity prices (especially iron ore and copper) and China's economy.
USD/CAD
Dollar vs Canadian Dollar. Called "Loonie." Strongly linked to oil prices — when oil rises, CAD usually rises too.
💡 Safe Haven Explained: A "safe haven" currency is one that people rush to BUY when the world feels dangerous or uncertain. Think of it like people buying gold when they are scared. The Japanese Yen (JPY), Swiss Franc (CHF), and sometimes the US Dollar (USD) are the main safe havens in forex.
If EUR/USD moves from 1.0800 to 1.0700, what happened?
The Euro got WEAKER against the Dollar
The Euro got STRONGER against the Dollar
Nothing changed — they are equal
LESSON 03 · FOREX BASICS
What Are Pips? How Forex Profits Are Measured
The unit of measurement every forex trader must know

A pip is the smallest standard unit of movement in a currency pair. It stands for "Percentage In Point". Most pairs move in pips of 0.0001 — that is the 4th decimal place.

📖 PIP EXAMPLE — EUR/USD

EUR/USD moves from 1.0850 to 1.0880

That is a move of 30 pips (0.0030)

On a standard lot (100,000 units), each pip = approximately $10

So 30 pips = $300 profit

// Lot Sizes — How Much You Are Trading
Standard Lot
100,000 units of currency. Each pip = ~$10. For experienced traders with large accounts.
Mini Lot
10,000 units. Each pip = ~$1. Good for intermediate traders.
Micro Lot
1,000 units. Each pip = ~$0.10. Perfect for beginners — very low risk while learning.
Spread
The difference between the buy price and sell price. This is what your broker charges. A spread of 1.2 pips on EUR/USD means you start each trade 1.2 pips in the negative.
⚠ Important: Always start with micro lots when you are learning. A 50 pip loss on a micro lot is only $5. The same loss on a standard lot is $500. Keep your losses small while you are developing your skills.
📖 CALCULATING YOUR PROFIT/LOSS

You buy EUR/USD at 1.0850 with 1 mini lot (10,000 units)

Price moves to 1.0920 — you close the trade

Movement = 70 pips × $1 per pip = $70 profit

If price moved DOWN to 1.0780 — that is 70 pips against you = $70 loss

LESSON 04 · FOREX BASICS
Market Sessions — When To Trade
Forex runs 24/5 — but not all hours are equal

Forex runs 24 hours a day, Monday to Friday. But there are four major trading sessions when different banks and traders around the world are active. The best trading opportunities happen when sessions overlap — that is when volume and volatility are highest.

🇦🇺 Sydney Session
10 PM – 7 AM EST. Quietest session. Low volume. AUD and NZD pairs most active. Not ideal for beginners.
🇯🇵 Tokyo Session
7 PM – 4 AM EST. JPY pairs most active. Can be slow. Watch for Bank of Japan news. USD/JPY and GBP/JPY move here.
🇬🇧 London Session ⭐
3 AM – 12 PM EST. MOST VOLATILE session. 40% of all daily forex volume. EUR, GBP pairs are very active. Best for serious traders.
🇺🇸 New York Session ⭐
8 AM – 5 PM EST. Second most active. US economic data releases move the market big. USD pairs are most active.
⭐ The Golden Window: The best time to trade is the London/New York overlap: 8 AM – 12 PM EST. Both major markets are open at the same time, volume is massive, and pairs like EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY make their biggest moves of the day here.
LESSON 05 · FOREX BASICS
Leverage & Margin — The Double-Edged Sword
How traders control large positions with small accounts — and why it is dangerous

Leverage means your broker lets you control a much larger trade than your account balance. For example, with 100:1 leverage, you can control $100,000 worth of currency with just $1,000 in your account.

📖 LEVERAGE EXAMPLE

You have $1,000 in your account

Your broker offers 50:1 leverage

You can trade up to $50,000 worth of currency

A 1% move in your favor = $500 profit (50% of your account!)

A 1% move against you = $500 loss (50% of your account wiped)

Margin is the deposit your broker holds as collateral while your trade is open. If your losses approach your margin, the broker will automatically close your trade — this is called a margin call.

⚠ The Most Important Warning: High leverage is how beginner traders blow up their accounts fast. Never use more than 10:1 leverage when starting out. Most professional traders use 5:1 or less. The goal is to stay in the game long enough to become profitable — not to get rich overnight.
✅ Rule of Thumb: Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. If you have $1,000, your maximum loss per trade should be $10-$20. This keeps you alive long enough to learn.
LESSON 06 · STOCKS BASICS
What Is a Stock?
How owning a piece of a company can make you wealthy

A stock (also called a share) is a small piece of ownership in a company. When you buy 1 share of Apple, you literally own a tiny fraction of Apple Inc. — including its profits, its assets, and its future growth.

Companies sell stock to raise money. Instead of borrowing from a bank, they sell pieces of ownership to thousands of investors. In return, investors hope the company grows and their shares become worth more over time.

📈 Simple Example: In 2003, Apple's stock was worth about $1 per share (adjusted for splits). By 2024, it was worth over $190. If you had invested $1,000 in 2003, it would be worth over $190,000 today — without doing anything except holding.
// How Stock Prices Move
1
Earnings Reports: Every 3 months, companies report their profits. If they beat expectations, the stock usually jumps. If they miss, it drops. This is the biggest short-term driver of individual stocks.
2
News & Announcements: New products, partnerships, lawsuits, CEO changes — any major news can move a stock significantly in minutes.
3
Market Sentiment: When the overall market is feeling positive (RISK-ON), most stocks rise together. When fear takes over (RISK-OFF), most stocks fall. This is why the VIX (Fear Index) matters.
4
Interest Rates: When the Fed raises rates, it becomes more expensive to borrow money — this slows down companies and their growth projections, which tends to push stock prices lower.
LESSON 07 · STOCKS BASICS
How The Stock Market Works
The NYSE, NASDAQ, SPY, VIX — what they all mean

The stock market is where buyers and sellers meet to trade shares of companies. The two biggest stock exchanges in the world are the NYSE (New York Stock Exchange) and NASDAQ. Both are based in New York and open 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM EST, Monday to Friday.

SPY / S&P 500
A basket of the 500 biggest US companies. When people say "the market is up," they usually mean the S&P 500. It is the best measure of the overall US stock market health.
VIX — Fear Index
Measures how scared or calm the market is. VIX above 30 = extreme fear, stocks usually falling. VIX below 15 = calm, stocks usually rising. Wolf Terminal shows this in every scan.
Bull Market
When prices are rising and people are optimistic. A 20% rise from a low is officially a bull market. 2023-2024 was a strong bull market driven by AI hype.
Bear Market
When prices are falling and fear is high. A 20% drop from a high is officially a bear market. 2022 was a bear market caused by inflation and rising interest rates.
🐺 How Wolf Terminal Uses This: Every Wolf scan checks the SPY price, the VIX level, and the overall market regime (BULLISH / NEUTRAL / BEARISH) before making picks. When VIX is above 25, the scanner becomes more conservative. When the market is in a BULLISH regime, it looks for more aggressive BUY signals.
LESSON 08 · STOCKS BASICS
Options Explained Simply
What calls and puts are — and why traders love them

An option is a contract that gives you the RIGHT to buy or sell a stock at a specific price, before a specific date. You pay a small premium upfront for this right.

CALL Option
Gives you the right to BUY a stock at a set price. You buy a CALL when you think the stock is going UP. If it does, your call gains value fast.
PUT Option
Gives you the right to SELL a stock at a set price. You buy a PUT when you think the stock is going DOWN. Puts are also used to protect (hedge) a portfolio.
Strike Price
The price at which you can exercise your option. If NVDA is at $100 and you buy a $105 CALL, the stock needs to reach $105 before your option is "in the money."
Expiration Date
Options expire. If the stock doesn't reach your strike price before expiration, the option expires worthless and you lose the premium you paid. Time is always working against you as a buyer.
📖 SIMPLE CALL OPTION EXAMPLE

NVDA is at $100. You think it will rise after earnings next week.

You buy 1 CALL option: Strike $105, Expiry in 7 days, Premium = $2.00 (cost: $200 for 1 contract of 100 shares)

NVDA jumps to $120 after earnings → Your $105 call is now worth ~$15 → You made $1,300 profit on $200 invested

NVDA stays at $100 or drops → Option expires worthless → You lose $200 maximum

💡 Why Options? Options give you massive leverage with defined risk. Your maximum loss is only what you paid (the premium). But your potential gain can be 5x, 10x, or more. This is why Wolf Terminal includes an options play recommendation with every stock pick.
LESSON 09 · READING THE MARKET
How To Read Charts — Candlesticks
The language every trader speaks — learn it once, use it forever

Every price chart is made up of candlesticks. Each candle tells you exactly what happened during that time period — whether it was 1 minute, 1 hour, or 1 day.

📖 HOW TO READ ONE CANDLE

Green / White Candle = Price CLOSED higher than it opened → Buyers were in control

Red / Black Candle = Price CLOSED lower than it opened → Sellers were in control

The Body = Distance between open and close price

The Wick (Shadow) = The highest and lowest point the price reached — but then rejected

Long upper wick = Price tried to go higher but got rejected → sellers pushed back

Long lower wick = Price tried to go lower but got rejected → buyers stepped in

// Key Candle Patterns To Know
Doji
Open and close are almost equal — tiny body, long wicks. Means the market is UNDECIDED. Often signals a reversal is coming. Pay attention when you see a Doji after a big trend.
Engulfing Pattern
A big candle that completely "swallows" the previous candle. Bullish Engulfing (green swallows red) = buyers taking over. Bearish Engulfing (red swallows green) = sellers taking over.
Hammer
Small body at the top, very long lower wick. Looks like a hammer. Means sellers tried to push price down but buyers rejected them hard. Usually a BULLISH signal at a support level.
Shooting Star
Small body at the bottom, very long upper wick. Means buyers tried to push price up but sellers rejected them. Usually a BEARISH signal at a resistance level.
💡 The Most Important Skill: You don't need to memorize every pattern. Learn to ask one question looking at any candle: "Who was in control — buyers or sellers — and where did they get rejected?" That question alone will teach you more than any pattern list.
LESSON 10 · READING THE MARKET
Support & Resistance — The Foundation of Every Trade
The price levels where buyers and sellers have fought before — and will fight again

Support is a price level where buyers have stepped in and stopped price from falling. Think of it as a floor. When price falls to this level, buyers show up and push it back up.

Resistance is a price level where sellers have stepped in and stopped price from rising. Think of it as a ceiling. When price rises to this level, sellers show up and push it back down.

🔑 The Golden Rule: When price breaks THROUGH a resistance level, that old resistance often becomes the new support. When price breaks BELOW a support level, that old support often becomes the new resistance. This is called a "role reversal" and it is one of the most powerful concepts in all of trading.
📖 HOW TO USE SUPPORT & RESISTANCE

Buying at Support: Wait for price to come down and touch a known support level. Look for a bullish candle pattern (like a hammer or engulfing) to confirm buyers are defending it. Enter with a stop loss just below the support level.

Selling at Resistance: Wait for price to rise and touch a known resistance level. Look for a bearish candle pattern (like a shooting star) to confirm sellers are rejecting it. Enter with a stop loss just above the resistance level.

LESSON 11 · READING THE MARKET
RSI, EMA & MACD — The Three Most Important Indicators
The tools that Wolf Terminal uses in every scan — now you will understand what they mean
// RSI — Relative Strength Index

RSI measures how fast and how much price has been moving. It gives you a number from 0 to 100.

RSI Above 70
OVERBOUGHT — The market has moved up too fast. It may be due for a pullback. Not a signal to short immediately, but a warning to be cautious about new buys.
RSI Below 30
OVERSOLD — The market has dropped too fast. It may be due for a bounce. Potential buying opportunity, especially at a known support level.
RSI 40–60
Neutral zone — no clear signal. The trend is not extreme in either direction. Wait for RSI to reach the extremes before acting on it alone.
RSI Divergence
When price makes a new high but RSI makes a lower high — this is a warning that momentum is fading. One of the most powerful reversal signals.
// EMA — Exponential Moving Average

An EMA is a line on the chart that shows the average price over a set number of periods. It smooths out the noise and shows the overall trend direction.

The Three EMAs Wolf Uses:
EMA 20 = Short-term trend (roughly 1 month). Price above EMA 20 = short-term bullish.
EMA 50 = Medium-term trend (roughly 3 months). The most watched moving average by institutions.
EMA 200 = Long-term trend (roughly 1 year). THE most important line on any chart. Price above EMA 200 = UPTREND. Price below = DOWNTREND. Every professional trader watches this.
// MACD — Momentum Indicator

MACD shows the relationship between two moving averages and helps identify when momentum is shifting. When the MACD line crosses above zero, it is bullish. When it crosses below, it is bearish. A MACD crossing above its signal line is a BUY signal. Crossing below is a SELL signal.

✅ How Wolf Terminal Uses These: Every scan checks if price is above or below EMA 200 (trend), RSI level (momentum), and MACD bias (direction). When all three align — price above EMA 200, RSI between 40-60, and MACD bullish — that is a high-confidence BUY setup.
LESSON 12 · READING THE MARKET
Trend Analysis — Trade With The Flow, Not Against It
The single most important concept in all of technical analysis

The most fundamental rule in trading: "The trend is your friend." Trying to trade against a strong trend is like trying to swim upstream — you might make progress sometimes, but you will exhaust yourself and likely get swept away.

1
Identify the Trend on the Higher Timeframe First: Always start by looking at the weekly or daily chart. Is price making higher highs and higher lows? That is an UPTREND. Lower highs and lower lows? That is a DOWNTREND.
2
Look for Entry on the Lower Timeframe: Once you know the daily trend is UP, drop to the 4-hour or 1-hour chart and wait for a pullback to a support level or EMA. That is your entry point — you are buying in the direction of the bigger trend at a better price.
3
Do Not Fight Institutional Money: Banks and hedge funds move the market. When they decide a trend is in place, they hold their positions for weeks or months. Your job is to identify what direction they are pushing and follow them — not fight them.
⚠ The Biggest Beginner Mistake: Trying to call the top or bottom of a move. "It has gone up too much, I am going to short it." This is how accounts get destroyed. A strong trend can continue much further than logic suggests. Trade WITH the trend until it clearly breaks — then reassess.
LESSON 13 · RISK & MINDSET
Risk Management — The Skill That Separates Winners From Losers
You cannot control whether a trade wins. You can always control how much you lose.

Here is the truth that nobody wants to hear when they start trading: even the best traders in the world are wrong 40-50% of the time. What makes them profitable is not that they win more often — it is that when they win, they win bigger than when they lose.

🔑 The Golden Formula: If you risk $100 to make $300 (3:1 reward to risk), you only need to be right 34% of the time to be profitable. You can be WRONG on more than half your trades and still make money. Risk management is everything.
// The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1-2% of your total account on any single trade. Here is why this is life-changing:

📖 THE MATH OF SURVIVAL

Account: $5,000 | Rule: Never risk more than 1% ($50) per trade

Even if you lose 10 trades in a row: You still have $4,500 — you are still in the game

Account: $5,000 | Bad habit: Risking 20% ($1,000) per trade

5 losses in a row: Account is at $0. Game over.

The trader who survives long enough to learn is the trader who eventually wins.

// Stop Loss — Your Best Friend

A stop loss is an order you place that automatically closes your trade if price moves against you by a certain amount. It is not optional — it is required if you want to survive as a trader.

✅ How To Set A Stop Loss: Place your stop loss at a level where your trade idea is WRONG. For a buy trade at a support level, the stop goes just below the support. If price breaks below support, your idea was wrong — and you exit with a controlled loss before it gets worse.
LESSON 14 · RISK & MINDSET
Trading Psychology — Why Smart People Lose Money
The emotional traps that destroy more accounts than bad analysis

You can understand every indicator, know every pattern, and still lose money — because when real money is on the line, emotions take over. The market is designed to make you emotional. Learning to trade your plan, not your feelings, is the hardest and most valuable skill in trading.

Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)
Chasing a trade after it already moved big because you are scared of missing the profit. Almost always results in buying the top or selling the bottom. If you missed it, the next setup is coming. Wait.
Revenge Trading
After a loss, jumping back into the market immediately to "win it back." You are now trading on emotion, not analysis. This is how small losses become account-destroying losses. Walk away after a bad trade.
Moving Your Stop Loss
When a trade goes against you, moving your stop loss further away to "give it more room." You are now changing the rules after the game started. This leads to massive losses. Honor your stop — always.
Overtrading
Taking too many trades because you are bored or want to make money faster. Quality over quantity — the best traders take 2-5 high-confidence trades per week, not 20 random ones per day.
🐺 Wolf's Mindset Rule: Before entering any trade, write down: Why I am entering. Where my stop loss is. Where my target is. If the trade hits my stop — it is not a loss, it is the cost of doing business. A professional trader treats losses the same way a business owner treats expenses. They are part of the operation.
